# Daily 15-Minute Pull-Up Accessory Program (NO PULL-UP BAR)
**Equipment:** Resistance Bands, Kettlebells  
**Primary Goal:** Improve strict pull-up capacity  
**Current Baseline:** 4 unbroken reps  
**Constraints:** No pull-up bar access  
**Integration:** Compatible with 2 running sessions + 2 CrossFit HIIT sessions weekly  

---

## Key Training Principles (Bar-Free)

- Vertical pulling is replaced with **banded lat-biased patterns**
- Grip endurance is trained via **kettlebell holds and carries**
- Scapular depression is emphasized using **straight-arm band work**
- Elbow flexors are trained with **high-volume, joint-friendly tension**
- All sessions remain **sub-maximal** to allow daily frequency

---

## Session Library (Rotate Daily)

| Session | 15-Min Workout | Pull-Up Limiter Addressed | Structure |
|--------|----------------|---------------------------|-----------|
| A | Band Lat Pulldown / KB Suitcase Hold / Band Face Pull | Lats + grip | 5 rounds: Pulldown 15, Hold 30s/side, Face Pull 15 |
| B | Band High Row (45°) / Band Curl | Vertical-pull substitute | EMOM 12: Min 1 Row 15, Min 2 Curl 15 |
| C | Band Straight-Arm Pulldown / Hollow Hold | Scapular depression | 4 rounds: Pulldown 20, Hollow 30s |
| D | KB Gorilla Row (light) / Band Pallof Press | Unilateral lat + core | 4 rounds: Row 10/side, Pallof 12/side |
| E | Band Pullover / Band Reverse Fly | Long-lever lat strength | 4 rounds: Pullover 15, Fly 20 |
| F | KB Front Rack Hold / Band Row (slow) | Postural endurance | 3 rounds: Rack Hold 45s, Row 20 (3-1-3) |
| G | Band Dislocates / Lat Stretch / Breathing | Recovery & tissue quality | Continuous flow |

---

## Weekly Integration Guide

| Primary Training Day | Recommended Sessions |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Running days | A, C, or G |
| CrossFit HIIT days | B, D, or F (≥6h separation preferred) |
| Fatigue-heavy day | G only |

---

## Progression Strategy (Bar-Free)

| Element | How to Progress |
|-------|----------------|
| Band pulldowns | Thicker band or slower eccentrics |
| Rows | Increase reps before resistance |
| Holds & carries | Extend time to 45–60s |
| Curls | Chase volume, not load |
| Frequency | 6–7 days/week at RPE 6–7 |

---

## Expected Carryover to Pull-Ups

| Adaptation | Transfer Benefit |
|-----------|------------------|
| Lat hypertrophy | Stronger initial pull |
| Scapular depression | Cleaner first rep |
| Grip endurance | Longer unbroken sets |
| Elbow resilience | Higher weekly pulling volume |

**Realistic Outcome:**  
When bar access returns, expect rapid neurological carryover; most athletes regain **+2–4 reps within 2–3 weeks** of reintroducing strict pull-ups.

---

## Execution Rules

- Never train to failure
- Maintain neutral spine at all times
- Prioritize quality lat engagement over load
- If elbows flare up, reduce curl volume by 30–40%

---

**Program Intent:**  
Maximize pull-up-specific strength and endurance despite zero access to a pull-up bar, while remaining fully compatible with endurance and HIIT training demands.
